Not liable to tax in any other country
What "not liable to tax" means This is stricter than paying zero tax. It means the country where you live has no personal income tax law at all. UAE, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, Qatar = genuinely not liable. If you live in the US or UK and file a return there — even if your tax bill is zero — you are still liable under their domestic law. Being exempt is not the same as not being liable.
Your country of residence has no personal income tax — not just that you personally owe zero.
✓ Living in Dubai, UAE → Yes (no income tax law) ✓ Living in Saudi Arabia, Bahrain → Yes ✗ Living in US on H1B or W-2 → No (liable to US tax) ✗ Living in UK, filing Self Assessment → No ✗ Singapore tax resident → No
